Crypto Currencies Are The Future Of Money

Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies have risen dramatically in popularity over the past decade. What is cryptocurrency, and why is it important?

What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a shared record of transactions that are tied together to create a chain. Blockchains can be used to keep track of all sorts of things, but Bitcoin first used blockchain to issue currency and keep track of transactions.

Blockchains are made of networks of computers which all audit each other, and work together to create consensus on which transactions should be included in each block. A block is just a bundle of all the trasactions that happened over the last few minutes or seconds.

What Are The Advantages of Cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurencies are decentralized systems, which means that there is no central authority in charge of each cryptocurrency. Traditional monetary systems all have central authorities that can control the distribution of the money, censor transactions, levy taxes, print new currency, and otherwise control the flow of money. Cryptocurrencies circumvent the need for central authorities which makes them more democratic and free.

Privacy and Security

Privacy and security are also something that should be considered when thinking about cryptocurrencies. Cryptocurrencies will generally fall into two separate categories when considering their overall privacy and security concerns. Early cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin provide a traceable ledger of each transition. This makes it less private, but more secure in the sense that money laundering and counterfeit operations are useless through transaction tracing by law enforcement agencies and regulators. On the other hand, newer cryptocurrencies like Monero are filling a demand for a more privacy-centric approach to keeping identities and transactions concealed. While this invites the risk of criminal activities, it also provides a desired feature as privacy becomes a growing concern in the virtual world.

Cryptography in Blockchain

Blockchain and cryptocurrency systems use asymmetric public key cryptography to make transactions between users secure. Each user has a pair or public and private keys that are related cryptograpically and allow for identity verification. Due to the nature of the cryptography sytems, it is mathematically difficult to guess another users private key. All public keys are publicly visible, and they are used to create transactions which can only be "signed" by the private key associated with that public key. Transactions belong to these public keys, which function like an address. Since these key pairs are generated randomly by putting random numbers into a hash function, there is no way to infer the identity of the owner of an account.
